# 20 Long-Form YouTube Video Outlines

Target length: 6–15 minutes. LF-01 through LF-13 are scheduled by the 90-day calendar (file 02); LF-14–LF-20 are an evergreen bank to slot in wherever a week needs a strong anchor. Titles here are working titles — see file 06 for title/thumbnail A-B options.

Every outline follows the same skeleton: **Cold open (0:00–0:30) → Setup → Core content → Proof/demo → Recap → CTA.** Keep the cold open free of intro music/logo stings — hook first, brand second.

---

**LF-01 — "What TrackDelta Actually Is (And What It Isn't)"** — *Week 1, Launch*
- Cold open: a raw, confusing telemetry graph on screen. "This is what everyone else gives you. Here's what we built instead."
- Setup: the problem — drivers have data, no interpretation, no engineer.
- Core: walk the five-layer pipeline (parse → extract → DNA → coaching → Delta's voice) in plain language, emphasizing the LLM is the voice, not the brain.
- Demo: live upload → full debrief walkthrough, unedited processing time shown.
- Recap: what it is (a debrief), what it explicitly isn't (a dashboard, a leaderboard, real-time coaching — yet).
- CTA: sign up free, 3 sessions/month, no card.

**LF-02 — "Inside a Real Driver DNA Report"** — *Week 2*
- Cold open: DNA radar chart animating in. "This is built entirely from telemetry. No quiz, no self-report."
- Setup: what Driver DNA measures — braking, throttle, steering, consistency, risk, pressure, learning.
- Core: walk each attribute with a real/illustrative example, explain the EWMA update logic in plain terms (recent sessions matter more, but the model remembers).
- Demo: show DNA evolving across 3 sequential sessions.
- Recap: DNA is never "finished" — it's a living model.
- CTA: upload a session, see your own DNA start forming.

**LF-03 — "Why Your Brake Point Feels Right But Isn't"** — *Week 3, Education*
- Cold open: a driver confidently describing their braking, then the data disagreeing.
- Setup: the gap between felt performance and measured performance.
- Core: brake point consistency, trail braking depth, modulation quality — explained with real trace examples.
- Demo: a debrief opportunity card built around a braking issue, full walkthrough of the recommendation.
- Recap: feel lies under pressure; data doesn't.
- CTA: get your own brake-point breakdown.

**LF-04 — "The Throttle Mistake Costing You a Second a Lap"** — *Week 4*
- Cold open: two nearly identical laps, one a second faster — same braking, different exits.
- Setup: why corner exit dominates lap time more than most drivers assume.
- Core: throttle application point, ramp rate, modulation vs. full-throttle fraction.
- Demo: a real opportunity card focused on throttle timing.
- Recap: smoother, not just earlier.
- CTA: check your own throttle application data.

**LF-05 — "One Month of Building TrackDelta in Public"** — *Week 5, Building in Public*
- Cold open: "One month ago, none of this existed."
- Setup: why building in public at all — accountability, and because sim racers can smell fake marketing instantly.
- Core: real timeline — Phase 0 infra, parser build, first real telemetry test, first real users, the bugs found along the way (including the DNA-loss race condition, told honestly).
- Demo: quick tour of what shipped.
- Recap: what's working, what isn't, what's next.
- CTA: follow along, feedback genuinely shapes the roadmap.

**LF-06 — "The Line Everyone Gets Wrong at [Track]"** — *Week 6, Education* (swap in whichever seeded track has the richest corner data)
- Cold open: two onboard laps through one corner, wildly different lines, similar lap time — until the next corner.
- Setup: the "ideal line" is a starting point, not a universal answer.
- Core: how entry speed, car balance, and personal braking style shift the truly optimal line for a specific driver.
- Demo: corner-level data for that specific track/corner from TrackDelta.
- Recap: track knowledge + personal data beats generic racing lines.
- CTA: upload a session at this track, compare.

**LF-07 — "iRating vs. Actually Being Fast"** — *Week 7*
- Cold open: a high-iRating driver who isn't actually the fastest in the room, and why that's normal.
- Setup: iRating measures results relative to a field; it doesn't measure raw pace or racecraft directly.
- Core: consistency score, risk profile, and racecraft as separate, measurable things from iRating.
- Demo: DNA consistency/risk sections explained.
- Recap: chase the skill, the rating follows.
- CTA: see your consistency score.

**LF-08 — "We Analyzed a Subscriber's Session Live"** — *Week 8, Community*
- Cold open: "A subscriber sent in their session. Let's see what Delta found — no edits."
- Setup: brief context on the driver (car, track, goal), with permission.
- Core: full live debrief read-through, reacting genuinely to each section.
- Demo: N/A — this whole video is the demo.
- Recap: what this driver should work on next.
- CTA: submit your own session for a future episode.

**LF-09 — "Why Delta Says 'I Don't Know'"** — *Week 9, Trust*
- Cold open: a debrief showing a suppressed estimate ("not enough data yet").
- Setup: the Truth Over Confidence principle, why it exists as a company value, not a UX guideline.
- Core: confidence scoring system end to end — what feeds it, thresholds, how coaching output gets gated by it.
- Demo: same driver's debrief at session 1 vs session 10, confidence visibly changing.
- Recap: trust compounds; false confidence erodes it once and permanently.
- CTA: read the full confidence spec (link to a blog/doc if published).

**LF-10 — "Shipping the Feature Everyone Asked For"** — *Week 10, Building in Public*
- Cold open: a real, aggregated user request read aloud.
- Setup: why this feature (Delta Conversations, DNA history charts, or whichever is actually shipping) got prioritized.
- Core: build story — design decisions, what got cut from v1, what's next.
- Demo: full walkthrough of the new feature.
- Recap: what feedback shaped this, what's next on the list.
- CTA: try the new feature, keep the feedback coming.

**LF-11 — "Qualifying Pace vs. Race Pace — Why They Differ"** — *Week 11*
- Cold open: same driver, wildly different quali and race gaps to the leader.
- Setup: fuel load, tire degradation, traffic, and the psychology of one hot lap vs. twenty.
- Core: pressure performance attribute — early vs late session pace delta, what it's built from.
- Demo: a driver's pressure-performance data (once available with sufficient session history).
- Recap: train both skills separately.
- CTA: see your own pressure profile once you've logged enough sessions.

**LF-12 — "This Driver Dropped 1.2 Seconds in 3 Weeks"** — *Week 12, Case Study*
- Cold open: the actual before/after lap time and track, on screen immediately.
- Setup: who this driver is (with permission), their starting point.
- Core: walk the 3-4 sessions, what Delta flagged each time, what the driver actually changed.
- Demo: side-by-side DNA snapshots across the arc.
- Recap: this is what "measurable improvement after 4+ sessions" — the actual North Star metric — looks like in one real case.
- CTA: this could be your next 3 weeks — start free.

**LF-13 — "90 Days, Real Numbers, What We Learned"** — *Week 13, Retrospective*
- Cold open: the honest headline number first (retention, conversion, whatever it actually is — good or not).
- Setup: what the 90-day plan was (reference the internal roadmap philosophy without over-exposing internals).
- Core: what worked (content pillars, features), what didn't, what surprised the team.
- Demo: N/A, or a quick highlight reel of shipped features.
- Recap: what the next 90 days look like.
- CTA: subscribe for the next chapter; try the product if you haven't.

## Evergreen Bank (LF-14–LF-20) — slot in wherever a week needs a strong anchor

**LF-14 — "The Complete Beginner's Guide to Reading Telemetry"**
Cold open with the most confusing possible raw trace → systematically build up literacy: speed trace, brake trace, throttle trace, steering trace, how they relate to the track map. Ends with "this is the literacy TrackDelta builds automatically, but understanding it yourself makes you a better driver regardless."

**LF-15 — "Trail Braking: The Complete Breakdown"**
Full technical + practical treatment: what it is, when it helps, when it hurts, how to practice it deliberately, how TrackDelta measures trail_braking_fraction and depth.

**LF-16 — "Consistency Is a Skill You Can Train — Here's How"**
Define consistency score/lap_time_cv, then give concrete practice methodology (single-focus practice sessions, the "one clear objective" philosophy) for improving it.

**LF-17 — "Racecraft: The Skills Lap Time Doesn't Measure"**
Defending, overtaking, tire/fuel management, race-reading. Position TrackDelta's current scope honestly (telemetry-based, session-level) alongside what racecraft coaching still requires a human/replay review for — credibility through honesty about scope.

**LF-18 — "How an AI Race Engineer Is Different From ChatGPT Reading Your Data"**
Directly addresses the skepticism: explain why raw telemetry never touches the LLM, why the engineering layer (parser → extractor → DNA → coaching engine) does the actual analysis, and the LLM only narrates structured output. A trust/technical-credibility video for a more skeptical or technical audience segment.

**LF-19 — "The Psychology of Getting Coached by Data Instead of a Person"**
Softer, more reflective video: why it's easier to hear hard feedback from data than from a person, why some drivers resist it anyway, how Delta's tone (calm, non-judgmental, evidence-based) is designed around that psychology.

**LF-20 — "What We're Building Next (And What We're Deliberately Not)"**
A roadmap-transparency video: what's coming (from the public-facing parts of the roadmap), and an honest list of things deliberately deferred (real-time coaching, other sims, mobile app) and why — reinforcing focus as a feature, not a limitation.

## Production Notes (applies to all 20)

- Cold open must play before any logo/intro sting — first 3 seconds decide retention
- Every on-screen data visual either real (with permission) or explicitly labeled illustrative
- Pin a comment with a one-line summary + link on every upload
- End screen always points to two things max: the free sign-up and one related video
- Chaptering (YouTube timestamps) required on anything over 8 minutes
