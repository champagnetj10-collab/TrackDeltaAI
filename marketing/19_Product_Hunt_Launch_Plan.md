# Product Hunt Launch Plan

Distinct from [07_Launch_Week_Content.md](07_Launch_Week_Content.md) (the social launch) — this is specifically for a Product Hunt listing, which has its own culture, timing rules, and success mechanics. Can run the same week as the social launch, or later once there's a stronger case study to lead with — see §6.

---

## 1. Should Launch Week Include Product Hunt, or Come Later?

Two valid approaches:
- **Same week:** maximizes combined noise, but PH's audience (broadly tech/startup, not sim-racing-specific) will convert at a much lower rate than the sim racing channels — most PH traffic won't be the target persona at all
- **Later (after real user traction):** lets the listing lead with real numbers/testimonials, which PH's comment culture rewards heavily; the honest recommendation is to treat PH as a **secondary, credibility/exposure play**, not the primary launch vehicle — this audience is competitive iRacing drivers, and PH's audience is only a loose superset of "people who like new software."

**Recommendation:** run it in Week 2-4, once at least a few real (permissioned) early users exist to comment/vouch authentically, rather than Day 1.

---

## 2. Pre-Launch Checklist (2 weeks out)

- [ ] Product Hunt maker account set up, profile complete, prior genuine participation on the platform (commenting/upvoting other launches) for at least a couple weeks before your own launch — cold accounts with zero history posting a launch out of nowhere read as inauthentic to the PH community
- [ ] Gallery assets prepared: 3-5 images/GIFs showing the actual product (debrief screen, DNA radar, upload flow) — use real screenshots, not marketing mockups
- [ ] A short (60-90s) demo video — can reuse/trim an existing long-form clip (e.g., from LF-01) rather than producing something new
- [ ] Tagline drafted (PH taglines are short, ~60 characters) — draft options in §4
- [ ] First comment (the "maker comment," posted by the founder immediately at launch) written in advance — see template §5
- [ ] A short list of people who've genuinely used the product and are willing to comment honestly on launch day (do not ask for fake/paid upvotes — PH actively detects and penalizes vote manipulation, and it's exactly the kind of dishonesty this brand can't afford anywhere)
- [ ] Hunter identified — if a well-known PH hunter agrees to submit it, it gets more initial visibility than a self-hunt; if not, self-hunting is completely fine and increasingly normalized

---

## 3. Launch Day Timing

- Product Hunt's day resets at 12:01 AM Pacific Time — submit right at reset for the longest possible window of visibility within that day's competition
- Have the founder available all day for comment replies — PH's culture rewards makers who are visibly, genuinely present answering questions in real time

---

## 4. Tagline Options (~60 characters)

- "An AI race engineer that actually knows how you drive"
- "Your iRacing telemetry, turned into a real debrief"
- "Driver DNA: an evolving model of how you drive, built by AI"
- "Stop reading lap graphs. Get a real coaching debrief."

---

## 5. Maker Comment Template (posted immediately at launch by the founder)

```
Hey Product Hunt 👋

I built TrackDelta AI because competitive sim racers (myself included) have more telemetry data available than ever, and almost no way to actually interpret it. Every tool shows you a graph. Almost none tell you what to do about it.

TrackDelta reads your iRacing telemetry (.ibt files) and builds Driver DNA — an evolving model of how you specifically drive — then delivers a coaching debrief in the voice of an AI race engineer we call Delta.

The principle I care most about: Delta will tell you when it doesn't know something yet, instead of faking confidence. That's not a tagline, it's an actual constraint in how the confidence scoring works.

Free to start, 3 sessions/month, no card required. Would genuinely love feedback from anyone here, sim racer or not — especially on anything that's confusing or feels off.

Happy to answer anything today.
```

---

## 6. What to Lead With If Launching Later (post-traction)

If following the recommended later timing, replace the maker comment's framing with a real, honest metric or case study lead-in ("X drivers have used this so far, here's a real story..." — only if genuinely true) — PH's audience responds well to real traction signals, and fabricating them is both against the brand's core principle and a fast way to get called out in the comments, which are unusually candid on this platform.

## 7. Post-Launch

- Cross-post the PH link to X/LinkedIn (building-in-public angle: "we launched on Product Hunt today, here's how it's going" — genuinely fits file 08's template 6, Milestone Post)
- Respond to every comment, including critical ones, in the brand voice (§9 of file 01 — real, specific answers, not canned thanks)
- Whatever rank/result happens, do an honest recap post afterward (good or modest result) — consistent with the brand's transparency principle, and it's genuinely interesting content either way
