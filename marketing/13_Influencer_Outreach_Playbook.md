For **who** to reach out to, see [10_Creator_Community_Hashtag_List.md](10_Creator_Community_Hashtag_List.md) (and re-verify that list before using it, per its own caveat). ## 1. Outreach Philosophy

Sim racing creators — especially the credible, technically-respected ones this product needs — get pitched constantly by low-effort sponsors. The single biggest differentiator available is **specificity and genuine value-first framing**. A generic "check out our app" DM gets ignored or deleted. A pitch that references something real about their content and offers something genuinely useful to *them* (not just exposure for TrackDelta) gets a reply.

**Never pay for a review before there's real product traction to point to.** Pre-traction, the only currency available is free access + a genuinely interesting content angle. Paid sponsorship conversations belong after there are real case studies, real user numbers, and real revenue to support it (see Tier definitions below).

## 2. Tiering & Approach (expands on file 10 §1)

| Tier | Outreach approach | What's offered |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (500k+) | Do not cold-outreach pre-traction. Get organically noticed via Tier 2/3 creators mentioning TrackDelta, real case studies, and community buzz. Revisit once there's a compelling, quantified case study to lead with. | N/A pre-traction |
| Tier 2 (20k–500k) | Warm outreach — engage with their content genuinely for 2+ weeks first, then a specific, personalized pitch (template below) | Free Pro access (lifetime or extended, negotiable), a fully pre-built Driver DNA report from one of their own real sessions as the pitch hook, first access to new features |
| Tier 3 (under 20k, coaches, league organizers) | Most receptive tier — direct outreach is reasonable without a long warm-up period, since the relationship is more peer-to-peer | Free Pro access, priority in the "we analyzed a subscriber's session" format (LF-08), co-promotion (share their content back) |

## 3. The Outreach Process (step by step)

1. **Research (30–60 min minimum per Tier 1/2 target):** watch/read enough of their actual content to reference something specific and true. Confirm current activity level and audience fit — re-verify anything sourced from file 10.
2. **Warm-up (Tier 2 especially):** genuinely engage with 3-5 of their posts/videos over 1-2 weeks — real comments, not just likes — before ever sending a pitch. This isn't manipulation, it's just being a real part of the space before asking for something.
3. **First contact:** send the pitch (template below) via the channel they've indicated they prefer (many creators list a business email in their bio — use that over DMs when available; it signals a more serious, respectful approach).
4. **Follow-up:** if no response after 7-10 days, one polite follow-up. If no response after that, stop — do not send a third message. Move to the next creator.
5. **If they respond positively:** send the offer clearly in writing (what they get, what — if anything — is asked of them), and make it genuinely easy: pre-fill a Pro account for them, offer to jump on a quick call if that's easier than text.
6. **If they say no or don't respond:** log it (tracking sheet below) and move on without pressure. Relationships in this space are long — a "no" now doesn't mean "no" once there's more traction to point to later.
7. **After a collab happens:** thank them publicly and genuinely (not just a transactional "thanks for the shoutout"), share their content back across TrackDelta's own channels, and check in a few weeks later with real results if there are any (e.g., how many sign-ups came from their audience, shared transparently with them too).

## 4. Outreach Message Templates

### Template A — Tier 2/3 Cold/Warm Email

```
Subject: A Driver DNA report from your own [track/car] session

Hi [Name],

I've been genuinely enjoying your [specific recent video/stream — reference something real].

I built TrackDelta AI — it reads iRacing telemetry and builds an evolving model of how a specific driver drives (we call it Driver DNA), then delivers a coaching debrief instead of just a graph.

I'd love to send you free Pro access, no strings attached. If you're ever up for it, I think a genuinely interesting video could come out of running one of your own real sessions through it live and reacting to what it finds — but there's zero obligation attached to the free access either way.

Either way, really appreciate what you're putting out for the iRacing community.

[Name]
Founder, TrackDelta AI
[link]
```

### Template B — Tier 3 Direct/Peer Outreach

```
Hey [Name] — saw your [league/content], really solid stuff.

I'm building TrackDelta AI, an AI race engineer for iRacing (reads your telemetry, gives you a real debrief instead of a raw graph). Would love to get you Pro access free if you want to try it on your own sessions — genuinely just want good, honest feedback from people who actually race competitively.

No pressure to post about it, but if you ever want to do a "reacting to my own debrief" style video, happy to help however's useful.

[link if helpful context]
```

### Template C — Post-Collab Thank You

```
Hey [Name] — really appreciate you covering TrackDelta, thank you genuinely.

[One specific, real thing you appreciated about how they covered it]

Sharing it across our channels now. If you ever want real numbers on how it performed with your audience, happy to share transparently — always want to make sure this is actually valuable for you, not just a one-way ask.
```

### Template D — Re-engagement (for a past "no" or non-response, used sparingly, months later)

```
Hey [Name] — no pressure at all, just wanted to follow up since it's been a while.

[One genuine, specific update — e.g., a real user milestone, a meaningful feature shipped] since we last talked.

Offer's still open if you're ever interested — either way, hope things are going well with [something specific to them].
```

## 6. What Never to Do

- Never send a mass, identical pitch to a list of creators — it's detectable and it's disrespectful of the actual research step
- Never offer paid compensation before there's real traction/revenue to sustain it honestly
- Never ask a creator to say something that isn't genuinely true about the product
- Never follow up more than twice without a real, new update to justify the message
- Never publicly call out or complain about a creator who declined or ignored an outreach attempt — this is a small, tightly networked community and that kind of behavior travels fast

## 7. Escalating to Paid Partnerships (later stage, not launch)

Once there's real traction (a compelling North Star metric number, real case studies, actual revenue), a formal paid partnership conversation becomes reasonable, especially for Tier 1/2 creators. At that point:
- Have a clear, honest one-pager: real user numbers, a genuine case study or two, clear terms (flat fee vs. affiliate/referral-code commission — see file 15)
- Prefer affiliate/referral structures over flat fees where possible early on — it aligns incentive with real results rather than paying for exposure regardless of outcome
- Get terms in writing even for informal arrangements — protects both sides
