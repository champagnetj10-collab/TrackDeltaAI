# Launch Week — Full Content Plan (Days 1–7)

This is the fully-detailed version of Week 1 from the [90-day calendar](02_90_Day_Content_Calendar.md). Goal for the week: introduce TrackDelta AI honestly, prove it's real (not vaporware) with a live product, and get the first cohort of real users through the door — not maximize views for their own sake.

**Pre-launch checklist (before Day 1):**
- [ ] All 6 platform handles secured as `@TrackDeltaAI` (or closest available, kept consistent across all)
- [ ] Profile pictures = logo mark, banners = brand gradient per the style guide
- [ ] Bio/about copy finalized on every platform (template below)
- [ ] Link-in-bio tool set up pointing to the actual sign-up page
- [ ] At least SF-01 through SF-06 fully edited and ready before Day 1 (don't launch and edit simultaneously)
- [ ] LF-01 recorded, edited, scheduled
- [ ] Founder available for real-time replies during launch week — this is the week engagement matters most

**Bio template (adapt per platform character limit):**
> TrackDelta AI — the AI race engineer that learns how you drive. Upload your iRacing telemetry, get a real debrief. Free to start. 🔗

## Launch Week Success Criteria

Borrowing directly from the PRD's qualitative success criteria — apply the same bar to launch week content, not just the product:
- At least one real, unprompted comment/DM saying some version of "this is different from what I've tried before"
- Real sign-ups attempted the full upload → debrief flow, not just landing-page bounces
- No content posted this week makes a claim the product can't actually back up

If Day 7 review shows a platform generated zero meaningful engagement, don't panic-abandon it after one week — but do flag it for a harder look at the 30-day checkpoint.
