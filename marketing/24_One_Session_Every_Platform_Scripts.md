# One Session → Every Platform: The Content Multiplication Script

A step-by-step process for turning a single real (permissioned) driver session into a full week's worth of content across every platform in this system, without repeating the same post verbatim everywhere. This is the practical "assembly line" that ties files 03/04/05/09/11/12/23 together into one workflow.

**Source event:** one real debrief — a driver's session, fully processed, with a headline, opportunities, strengths, a practice plan, and a DNA update.

---

## Step 1 — Extract the Raw Material (do this once, immediately after the debrief generates)

From the single debrief, pull out these distinct, reusable pieces:
1. The headline / session overview sentence
2. The single "one clear objective" line
3. The top opportunity (corner, finding, estimated gain, confidence)
4. The top strength
5. One practice plan item (drill + success metric)
6. The DNA update note (what changed in the driver's profile)
7. The lap chart (if there's a clean visual story — a steady improvement or a notable outlier lap)
8. Any session note the driver wrote themselves

That's 8 distinct raw materials from one event — more than enough for a full week without repetition.

---

## Step 2 — Map Each Piece to a Platform-Native Format

| Raw material | Best format | Platform(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline/overview | Quote-card image | X, Instagram Story |
| One clear objective | 15-20s vertical video, `TD-Stat` style single-line callout | TikTok, Shorts, Reels |
| Top opportunity | "Data Reveal" video (Template A, file 06) — corner name + estimated gain as the big number | YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Reels |
| Top strength | Short "what's working" video, warmer tone | Instagram Reels, TikTok |
| Practice plan item | Text post describing the drill + why it matters | LinkedIn (framed as a technique lesson) or X thread |
| DNA update note | "Confidence moved" style video (mirrors SF-102) | YouTube Shorts |
| Lap chart | Simple animated line reveal, single stat callout | X (native video/GIF), Instagram Reels |
| Driver's own session note | Quote-card or voiceover framing device for another piece above | Any — use as connective tissue, e.g. "the driver said X, here's what the data showed" |

---

## Step 3 — Sequence Across the Week (don't dump all 8 pieces on day one)

| Day | Platform | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | TikTok + Shorts + Reels | "One clear objective" video |
| Tue | X | Headline quote-card + short thread giving context |
| Wed | YouTube Shorts + TikTok | Top opportunity "Data Reveal" |
| Thu | Instagram Reels | Top strength video |
| Fri | LinkedIn | Practice plan item framed as a technique lesson (ties to file 09's education content, gives it a real-example anchor instead of being purely abstract) |
| Sat | X or Reddit (if genuinely relevant to an ongoing thread) | DNA update / lap chart, framed honestly as one real (permissioned) example |
| Following week | YouTube long-form | If the story is rich enough (a real before/after arc across multiple sessions from the same driver), this becomes the seed for an LF-08 or LF-12 style video |

---

## Step 4 — Write the Actual Posts (use the AI prompts in file 23 to accelerate this step)

For each mapped piece, run it through the relevant prompt in [23_AI_Video_Generation_Prompts.md](23_AI_Video_Generation_Prompts.md):
- Opportunity/strength pieces → Prompt 3 (Corner-by-Corner Opportunity brief) or Prompt 1 (Debrief → Short-Form Script)
- DNA update → Prompt 2 (Radar visual brief) if a radar is shown, or Prompt 1 for the script
- Headline → Prompt 4 (Delta's Voice line) if it's being read aloud, otherwise use verbatim as the quote-card text

Then apply the CapCut template (file 05) for any video piece, and the thumbnail/title bank logic (file 06) if any piece graduates into a long-form video.

---

## Step 5 — Permission & Labeling Checklist (apply every single time, no exceptions)

- [ ] Explicit permission obtained from the driver to use their real session/debrief in content
- [ ] Driver's name/handle anonymized or credited exactly per their preference (ask, don't assume either way)
- [ ] Any illustrative/placeholder data used instead (if permission isn't given) is clearly labeled as such on screen or in caption, per file 01 §6
- [ ] Nothing in any derived piece adds a claim, number, or detail beyond what the real debrief actually contained

---

## Why This Process Matters More Than It Looks

Most sim racing content accounts either (a) produce generic advice with no real proof behind it, or (b) burn through real examples too fast because they post the "whole story" in one video and have nothing left. This process is deliberately structured to get a full week of genuinely specific, evidence-based content out of a single real event — which is exactly the kind of content this brand's credibility depends on (file 01's core positioning: "why is this not just another lap-time graph?"). One real session, treated this way, out-produces a week of generic advice content every time.
