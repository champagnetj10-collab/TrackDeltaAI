# 50 Short-Form Video Ideas (YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Instagram Reels)

Numbered SF-01 through SF-50, referenced by the content calendar (file 02). Each includes a hook (first on-screen line — has to land in 1.5 seconds), a beat-by-beat script, a caption, and a CTA. Length target: 15–45 seconds unless noted. All on-screen data visuals should match the app's real visual language (see brand guide §6) — where sample data is used instead of a real driver's session, say so.

Storyboard/editing pattern for all of these lives in [05_CapCut_Editing_Template.md](05_CapCut_Editing_Template.md) — these scripts assume that template's scene structure (Hook → Problem → Reveal → Payoff → CTA).

## Batch 4 (SF-31–40): Education Quick-Hits (braking / throttle / lines / racecraft)

**SF-31 — "Late braking isn't actually fast braking"** (Education, 25s)
- Hook: "Braking later doesn't automatically make you faster. Here's the actual mechanic."
- Script: 1) Explain: braking later without adjusting entry speed just means locking up or missing the apex. 2) "It's brake point *plus* modulation, not brake point alone."
- Caption: "Late braking is a tool, not a trophy. #SimRacingEducation #TrackDeltaAI"
- CTA: "More of this — follow."

**SF-32 — "Trail braking in 20 seconds"** (Education, 20s)
- Hook: "Trail braking, actually explained, no jargon."
- Script: 1) Define: carrying light brake pressure past turn-in, blending into the apex. 2) Show why it helps rotate the car in slower corners. 3) One-line caution: not every corner wants it.
- Caption: "The most misunderstood technique in sim racing. #SimRacingEducation"
- CTA: "Follow for more."

**SF-33 — "Throttle application isn't just 'floor it'"** (Education, 25s)
- Hook: "The exit of the corner is where most lap time actually lives."
- Script: 1) Explain throttle application point relative to apex. 2) Explain modulation vs. binary on/off. 3) "Smooth beats aggressive, almost always."
- Caption: "Corner exit > corner entry, most of the time. #SimRacingEducation #TrackDeltaAI"
- CTA: "Follow for more."
