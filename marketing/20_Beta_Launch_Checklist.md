# Beta Launch Checklist (Marketing/GTM Side)

This is the **marketing and go-to-market** checklist for closed beta. The **engineering readiness** checklist (monitoring, error handling, DNA validation across real sessions, etc.) already lives in [ROADMAP.md](../ROADMAP.md) under M3 (Closed Beta) — this document doesn't repeat that; it assumes M3's engineering gate is being worked in parallel and covers everything on the marketing side that needs to be true before or during that same window.

---

## 1. Before Recruiting Any Beta Driver

- [ ] Content foundation exists: brand voice guide (file 01), at least the launch-week content (file 07) drafted or published, and the profile/bio setup across platforms complete
- [ ] A real, monitored feedback channel exists (per ROADMAP.md M3, this might be as simple as a monitored email or the future Discord server — file 14 — brought forward earlier than its normal growth-stage trigger specifically to serve as the beta feedback channel)
- [ ] The 3-question post-first-debrief survey (per the PRD's beta plan) is actually built/ready to send — coordinate with whoever owns the email sequence (file 18) since this could piggyback on Email 3's timing
- [ ] Legal/privacy basics are in place enough to honestly tell a beta driver what happens to their data (ties to ROADMAP.md M4's Terms of Service / Privacy Policy gate — if those aren't live yet, beta communications need to say so honestly rather than imply they exist)

---

## 2. Recruiting the Beta Cohort

Per the PRD's beta plan (10 competitive iRacing drivers, 1,500–4,000 iRating, active league racers):

- [ ] Source candidates from: genuine participation in communities (file 10 §3), Tier 3 creator relationships already warmed up (file 13), and the founder's own network
- [ ] Personal, specific outreach to each candidate — not a mass callout post; use a version of file 13's Template B (Tier 3 direct outreach) adapted to explicitly frame it as a beta invitation with a real ask (4+ sessions over 3 weeks, honest feedback)
- [ ] Set expectations honestly up front: this is early, things may break, feedback directly shapes the product — matches the brand voice's "self-aware about being early" principle (file 01 §4)
- [ ] Confirm each beta driver knows how to reach the team directly (not just generic support) — the founder should be personally reachable during this phase

---

## 3. During the Beta Window

- [ ] Weekly check-in rhythm with the cohort (can be as light as a single message per week) — don't let 10 real people go quiet for weeks with no contact
- [ ] Capture real feedback as it comes in, tagged/organized for use in Building in Public content (file 08 template 5) *with explicit permission* before ever quoting anyone
- [ ] Track the actual PRD beta success criteria as they develop:
  - 8 of 10 return after first debrief
  - 6 of 10 show measurable lap-time improvement
  - At least 3 unprompted "this is different" reactions
  - No critical bug blocks a debrief from completing
- [ ] If a critical bug does block a debrief, communicate honestly and promptly with the affected driver(s) — this is a trust-building moment, not just a support ticket

---

## 4. Content to Prepare Alongside the Beta (don't publish until you have real material)

- [ ] Draft (don't publish) a version of LF-08 ("We Analyzed a Subscriber's Session Live") using early beta feedback as inspiration once a driver is willing
- [ ] Draft (don't publish) the case-study long-form outline (LF-12 style) — hold until there's a genuine, permissioned improvement story from the cohort
- [ ] Keep a running honest log of beta learnings for the eventual 90-day retrospective content (LF-13)

---

## 5. Exit Criteria — Moving From Beta to Public/Paid Launch

Do not move to the paid-launch marketing push (heavier Product Hunt push, wider creator outreach, paid acquisition if ever considered) until:
- [ ] PRD beta success criteria are genuinely met (not stretched or reinterpreted to count as "close enough")
- [ ] ROADMAP.md's M4 (Paid Launch Readiness Gate) is actually cleared on the engineering/business side — pricing finalized, Stripe tested live, legal pages published, support channel functioning
- [ ] At least one real, permissioned case study exists and is ready to lead with in launch content

If beta results are mixed (e.g., 5 of 10 return instead of 8), the honest move — consistent with everything else in this system — is to say so internally, understand why, and adjust before scaling marketing spend/effort behind a product that hasn't yet proven the North Star metric, not to quietly lower the bar and launch anyway.
