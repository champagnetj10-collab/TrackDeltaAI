# High-Value Sim Racing Creators, Communities & Hashtags

**Important caveat before using this list:** the creators and communities below are drawn from general knowledge of the sim racing content landscape and may be out of date — channels rebrand, go inactive, change focus, or move platforms. **Verify current handle, activity level, and audience fit directly before any outreach or collab pitch.** Treat this as a well-informed starting shortlist to research, not a verified, ready-to-message contact list.

---

## 1. Creator Tiers & Outreach Strategy

| Tier | Approx. reach | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Major sim racing channels** | 500k+ subs/followers | Long-shot cold outreach; more realistic path is getting organically noticed once there's real user traction/case studies to point to. Don't lead with a hard sponsorship ask pre-launch. |
| **Tier 2 — Established iRacing-focused creators** | 20k–500k | Best near-term collab target. Pitch: free Pro access + a genuine "react to your own Driver DNA" video concept (this is inherently good content for them, not just an ad). |
| **Tier 3 — Rising/niche iRacing creators, league organizers, coaches** | Under 20k | Highest response-rate tier and often the most engaged, credible audience per-follower. Great for the "we analyzed a subscriber's session" format (LF-08) — many will submit their own footage happily. |
| **Communities (non-creator)** | N/A | Organic participation first, promotion second — see §3 rules. |

**General collab pitch structure (adapt per creator):**
1. Specific — reference something real about their content, not a copy-paste opener
2. Lead with value to them (free Pro access, a genuinely interesting video concept, first access to a new feature) before mentioning TrackDelta needs anything from them
3. Make it easy: offer to send a pre-filled Driver DNA report from one of their own real sessions as the hook for the collab, not just "please talk about our app"

## 3. Communities to Engage (organically, before any promotion)

| Community | Platform | Engagement approach |
|---|---|---|
| r/simracing | Reddit | Participate genuinely in discussions for weeks before ever mentioning the product; when you do, disclose you're the founder/builder plainly — undisclosed self-promotion gets communities (and this one especially) to turn hostile fast |
| r/iRacing | Reddit | Same approach — this subreddit in particular skews toward the exact target persona (competitive iRacing drivers) |
| Overtake.gg (formerly RaceDepartment) forums | Forum | iRacing-specific subforums; long-form, detail-tolerant audience — good fit for technical deep-dive content style |
| iRacing official forums | Forum | More conservative community norms; read the room before any promotional post, check forum rules on third-party tool promotion specifically |
| League/team Discord servers | Discord | Many competitive leagues run their own Discords — genuine participation (helping other drivers, not drive-by promotion) is the only sustainable way in; ask league organizers directly about their self-promotion policy before posting anything |
| Trading Paints community | Discord/Web | Adjacent but relevant iRacing-enthusiast community — livery/car-customization focused, still same core driver base |

**The one rule for all of these:** show up as a person who's genuinely part of the hobby before showing up as a brand. This audience actively polices low-effort self-promotion, and a single bad first impression in a tight-knit community is hard to undo.

## 4. Hashtag Bank

**Own/branded (use on every post):**
`#TrackDeltaAI` `#DriverDNA`

**Core sim racing / iRacing (rotate 2-4 per post, don't stack all of them):**
`#iRacing` `#SimRacing` `#SimRacer` `#iRacingCommunity` `#VirtualRacing` `#EsportsRacing` `#RaceEngineer` `#Telemetry` `#DataDrivenRacing`

**Series/car-class specific (use only when the content is actually about that class):**
`#GT3` `#GT4` `#NASCARiRacing` `#IMSA` `#FormulaSimRacing` `#OpenWheelRacing`

**Track-specific (use when the video is genuinely about that track):**
`#WatkinsGlen` `#Spa` `#Nurburgring` `#RoadAmerica` (match to whichever tracks are seeded/discussed)

**Building in public / indie SaaS (LinkedIn/X only, not the video platforms):**
`#BuildInPublic` `#IndieHacker` `#SaaS` `#StartupJourney`

**What to avoid:**
- Real-world motorsport series hashtags (`#F1`, `#NASCAR` without "iRacing") unless the content is specifically about that series' iRacing presence — using them to bait unrelated audiences reads as spammy and can actively damage credibility with the real target audience
- Generic engagement-bait tags (`#fyp`, `#viral`, `#trending`) — platforms' own algorithms weight genuine niche relevance more than tag-stuffing for this kind of audience anyway
