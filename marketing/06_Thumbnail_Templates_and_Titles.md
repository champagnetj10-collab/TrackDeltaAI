# YouTube Thumbnail Templates & Title Bank

Thumbnails and titles are a matched pair — never finalize one without the other. Read [01_Brand_Voice_and_Style_Guide.md](01_Brand_Voice_and_Style_Guide.md) §6 for exact color values first.

---

## 1. Thumbnail Rules (apply to every upload)

- **1280×720px**, safe text zone within the center ~1150×620 (YouTube UI can crop edges on some devices)
- **Background:** Carbon Black `#0B0D10` or Graphite `#111317` — never a busy real-world photo background; keep it product/data-forward
- **One focal visual only** — a face, a data graphic, or a before/after split. Never all three at once.
- **Text on thumbnail:** 3-5 words max, Space Grotesk Bold, White with a Delta Blue `#0D6EFD` accent word or underline. Thumbnail text should NOT just repeat the video title verbatim — it should be the "hook fragment," the title fills in the rest.
- **Never:** red arrows, circles around faces, fake shocked-face reaction thumbnails, misleading numbers not actually in the video. This audience is data-literate and punishes clickbait harder than average.
- **Brand consistency:** logo mark small in a corner (same corner every time, bottom-right recommended, ~48px), Delta Blue accent bar or glow somewhere in every thumbnail so the channel is recognizable at a glance in a subscription feed.

## 2. The 4 Reusable Thumbnail Templates

### Template A — "Data Reveal"
Layout: dark background, one large data visual (DNA radar, corner-delta card, or lap chart) center-right, 3-word text top-left in `TD-Hook` style.
Use for: LF-02, LF-03, LF-04, LF-06, LF-09, LF-11, LF-15, LF-16 (any video where the core content IS a specific data visual).

### Template B — "Split Comparison"
Layout: vertical or diagonal split — left side "before/confusing/generic," right side "after/TrackDelta/specific." Bold "VS" or arrow between them in Apex Orange (the one place orange is a strong accent, since it's a rare A/B contrast case).
Use for: LF-01, LF-07, LF-18, LF-19, any before/after case study (LF-12).

### Template C — "Founder / Talking Head"
Layout: real face (founder or creator), shot against a dark/blurred background, positioned right third, text filling the left two-thirds in `TD-Hook` style, Delta Blue glow rim-light on the subject if possible.
Use for: building-in-public videos (LF-05, LF-10, LF-13, LF-20), any "here's the honest truth about X" framing.

### Template D — "Big Number"
Layout: one enormous stat/number as the dominant visual element (e.g. "1.2s", "90 DAYS", "$0"), Delta Blue or White, minimal supporting text, dark background.
Use for: LF-12 (case study), LF-13 (retrospective), any video whose entire premise is one impressive/honest number.

## 4. Short-Form Caption-as-Title Conventions (Shorts/TikTok/Reels don't get a separate "title" the way long-form does — the on-screen hook text doubles as the discovery hook)

Keep the file-03 hook lines as-is for on-screen text; for the platform title field (YouTube Shorts still has a title field even though it's less prominent than long-form), reuse the caption's first sentence, trimmed to under 60 characters where possible for full visibility in feed UI.

---

## 5. A/B Testing Discipline

- Never ship a long-form video without at least the two title options above drafted — pick the stronger one before publish, don't rely on YouTube's built-in A/B test feature alone (it needs volume this channel won't have early on)
- Re-evaluate thumbnail/title only in the first 48 hours if click-through is clearly underperforming similar videos — after that, let it ride; constant post-publish swapping confuses the algorithm more than it helps
