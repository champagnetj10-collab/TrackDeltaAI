# TrackDelta AI — Social Media Brand Voice & Style Guide

**Purpose:** every post, script, caption, and reply across every platform should sound like it came from the same place. This document is the reference every other file in `/marketing` assumes you've read.

---

## 1. The One-Sentence Brief

TrackDelta AI is the world's first AI race engineer that understands *the driver*, not just the data. **Delta** is the AI race engineer. **Driver DNA** is the evolving model of how a specific person drives. Social content's job is to make competitive iRacing drivers believe — correctly — that this is different from a telemetry dashboard.

---

## 2. Who We're Talking To

**Primary audience:** competitive iRacing drivers, 1,000–5,000 iRating. Active league racers, time trial competitors, esports aspirants. They already know MoTeC-style telemetry viewers exist. They've looked at their own lap traces and still don't know what to *do* about them. They have no personal coach and no race engineer.

**What they respond to:** specificity, real data, real corners, real lap times. They are allergic to generic sim-racing hype ("SEND IT 🔥🔥🔥") and allergic to corporate SaaS marketing speak. They trust other drivers and data over ads.

**What they're skeptical of:** another telemetry tool that's just MoTeC with a paint job. Every piece of content needs to answer, explicitly or implicitly: *why is this not just another lap-time graph?*

---

## 3. Delta's Character (governs first-person copy, video voiceover, and any post written "as Delta")

Delta is a calm, professional, trusted race engineer. Never a hype-man, never a chatbot.

**Delta is:**
- Honest — never invents data, never overstates confidence
- Specific — references real corners, real numbers, real laps
- Calm — same tone whether the session was great or rough
- Evidence-based — every claim traces to something measurable

**Delta is never:**
- Enthusiastic filler ("Great session today!! 🔥")
- Vague ("Try to improve your braking")
- Overconfident ("You have exactly 0.82 seconds available")
- A chatbot voice ("As an AI, I should note...")

Use Delta's first-person voice sparingly and deliberately — mainly in product-demo clips where Delta is literally narrating a debrief, or in captions styled as a "Delta's note." Don't let every account post become Delta; the *brand* account has a slightly wider, more human voice (see below), while *Delta the character* stays in the narrower lane above.

## 4. Brand Account Voice (the human-run handle, not Delta speaking)

Think: a sharp, experienced sim racing engineer talking to other racers on Discord — not a marketing department talking to "users."

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Turn 7. Same brake point, three sessions in a row. That's not bad luck, that's a pattern." | "🚨 Are YOU making this braking mistake?? 🚨" |
| "Delta doesn't guess. If the data's not there yet, it says so." | "Our revolutionary AI-powered technology..." |
| Cite an actual iRating range, car, or track when possible | Vague "drivers everywhere are loving this" claims |
| Short declarative sentences. Let data do the emphasis. | Excessive emoji, exclamation-point stacking |
| Confidence language: "likely," "based on this session," "early read" | False certainty: "guaranteed," "will make you faster" |
| Self-aware about being early / a small team | Overpromising like a funded-at-scale SaaS company |

**Reading level:** written for someone who already races competitively — you can use terms like apex, trail braking, iRating, SOF, without defining them in adult-facing copy. Define them only in explicitly educational content (see file 09).

---

## 5. Words We Use / Words We Avoid

**Use:** Delta, Driver DNA, debrief, coaching, confidence, corner-level, Truth Over Confidence, brake point, trail braking, throttle application, consistency, iRating, session, telemetry, race engineer.

**Avoid:** "gamify," "revolutionize," "AI-powered" (state what it *does* instead of branding the buzzword), "unlock your potential," "game-changing," "disrupt." If a sentence could appear in any generic SaaS launch tweet with the product name swapped out, rewrite it.

**Tagline usage:** "Discover your edge." is the primary brand tagline (logo lockups, bios, sign-offs). "Every lap is an opportunity." is a secondary line usable in CTAs and captions. Don't use both in the same post — pick one.

## 6. Visual Identity Quick Reference

(Full spec lives in the brand guide; this is the subset content creators need day to day.)

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Delta Blue | `#0D6EFD` | Primary accent — text highlights, graphic overlays, CTA buttons in thumbnails |
| Apex Orange | `#FF6800` | Secondary accent only — used sparingly for warnings, contrast callouts, "before" states |
| Carbon Black | `#0B0D10` | Background |
| Graphite | `#111317` | Card/panel surfaces |
| Steel | `#E5E7EB` | Body text on dark |
| Telemetry Gradient | Blue → Purple | Used behind data visuals, radar charts, hero art |
| Font | Space Grotesk | All on-screen text overlays, thumbnails, lower thirds |
| Logo mark | Triangle/arrow + speed trail + track node | App icon, watermark corner on every video, profile picture across all platforms |

**On-screen graphics rule:** any telemetry, DNA radar, or corner-delta visual shown in a video should visually match what's actually in the product (same color coding: green = time gained, orange/red = time lost). Never invent a different visual language for marketing than what the app shows — the moment someone signs up, the product should look exactly like what they saw in the video.

**Data disclosure rule:** if a graphic uses illustrative/sample data rather than a real driver's session, say so on-screen in small text ("Illustrative data") or in the caption. This is a direct extension of Truth Over Confidence — the marketing has to hold the same standard the product does.

## 7. Platform Tone Deltas

Same voice, different formality dial:

| Platform | Tone adjustment |
|---|---|
| **YouTube (long-form)** | Most explanatory, most patient. Full sentences, teaching cadence. |
| **YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Reels** | Fast, punchy, hook in the first line. Still no hype-emoji spam. |
| **X (Twitter)** | Terse, confident, data-forward. Good place for Delta "quote card" screenshots. |
| **LinkedIn** | Founder/building-in-public voice. First person, more candid about the business, still zero corporate-speak. |
| **Instagram Reels** | Same as TikTok/Shorts but captions can run slightly longer since IG users read more. |
| **Facebook** | Lowest priority platform — repurpose Reels/IG content, lean on the sim racing Facebook group ecosystem, slightly more explanatory captions since group audiences skew a bit older/less algorithm-native. |

## 8. Hashtag & Handle Conventions

- Primary handle across platforms: **@TrackDeltaAI**
- Primary hashtag: **#TrackDeltaAI**
- Always pair with 2-4 sim-racing-specific tags, never a wall of 20 (see file 10 for the working list)
- Never use motorsport hashtags tied to real-world series (F1, NASCAR) unless the post is genuinely about that series' iRacing content — don't hashtag-bait unrelated audiences

---

## 9. Comment & Reply Voice

Whoever runs the account replies as the brand voice (section 4), not as Delta. If someone asks a specific technical question about their own driving in the comments, it's fine to give a real, specific answer — that's exactly the credibility this brand needs. Never reply with a canned "Thanks for watching! 🙏" — either give a real answer or don't reply.
