# Platform-Specific Strategies

Deep, tactical, per-platform guidance. This goes beyond the cadence table in [02_90_Day_Content_Calendar.md](02_90_Day_Content_Calendar.md) §2 — that file tells you *how often*; this tells you *how to actually win the algorithm and the audience* on each one, including Reddit, which is new to the system.

---

## TikTok

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** TikTok weighs completion rate and rewatches above almost everything else, then shares/saves, then comments, then likes. It tests every video to a small initial audience regardless of follower count — a small account can outperform a big one on a single video if the hook holds.

**Format specifics:**
- First 1.5 seconds must communicate the topic without needing sound (many users scroll with sound off initially)
- Native trending sounds boost initial distribution, but never at the cost of the hook line's clarity — mute the trend under the voice, never over it
- Optimal length for this content type: 21–34 seconds (long enough to deliver a real insight, short enough to sustain full completion rate)
- On-screen captions mandatory (see CapCut template, file 05)

**Posting cadence & timing:** 5–7/week. Sim racers skew evening/late-night in their own timezone (post-work/school gaming hours) and weekend afternoons — test both a weekday-evening slot and a Saturday-midday slot in the first month, then follow whichever the analytics (file 21) actually show.

**Comment strategy:** Reply to the first 10-20 comments within the first hour of posting — early engagement velocity meaningfully affects distribution in the first few hours' testing window.

**What performs here specifically:** myth-busting (batch 6, file 11), reaction/duet/UGC challenge formats (batch 9), and culture/relatable content (batch 15) — TikTok's native culture rewards personality and participation formats more than the more polished YouTube-style product-proof content.

## Instagram Reels

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** Instagram weighs shares (especially to Stories/DMs) very heavily, then saves, then watch time. Reels get distributed beyond followers similarly to TikTok, but the "send to a friend" behavior is a bigger lever here than on TikTok.

**Format specifics:** Same 9:16 video works across TikTok/Reels/Shorts (this is why the CapCut master template exists) — but IG captions can run slightly longer since IG users read more in-feed than TikTok users. Add 1-2 sentences of extra context in the caption vs. what you'd write for the same video on TikTok.

**Posting cadence & timing:** 4–5/week. Slightly favor content worth sharing 1:1 (a specific corner callout a teammate would tag their friend in, a relatable moment) over pure education for this platform specifically.

**Distribution extras:** Always also post to Stories (with a link sticker where available) and consider a static-image carousel version of high-performing Reels content (e.g., the quote-card format from file 07) since IG's carousel format has its own separate audience/algorithm behavior.

**What performs here specifically:** Product-proof visuals (radar charts, corner-delta cards — these are inherently "pretty," shareable, screenshot-native content) and culture/relatable content.

## YouTube (Shorts + Long-Form)

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** YouTube's two surfaces behave differently. Shorts distribution resembles TikTok (completion rate-driven, tested to non-subscribers). Long-form is driven by session time (does this video keep someone on YouTube afterward, via suggested videos) and click-through rate on the thumbnail/title pairing — meaning file 06's thumbnail/title work directly determines long-form success more than on any other platform.

**Format specifics (long-form):**
- Cold open before any intro branding (see file 04's production notes)
- Chaptering required over 8 minutes
- Pinned comment with a one-line summary + link, posted immediately on publish
- End screen: two elements max (subscribe + one related video), never a cluttered end screen

**Format specifics (Shorts):** Same rules as TikTok apply for the Shorts feed itself, but cross-post the identical file rather than creating platform-unique edits for Shorts — the audience overlap with TikTok/Reels followers is usually low enough that re-use doesn't feel repetitive to most viewers.

**Posting cadence & timing:** 1 long-form/week (consistent day — pick one and never miss it, YouTube rewards a subscriber habit forming around a schedule), 5-7 Shorts/week.

**SEO note:** Long-form titles/descriptions are genuinely searchable (see file 16) — YouTube functions partly as a search engine for this content, unlike TikTok/Reels which are almost entirely discovery-feed driven.

**What performs here specifically:** Education content (batches from file 09 and file 11's batch 7/8) for long-form and search discovery; Product Proof and Building in Public for Shorts.

## X (Twitter)

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** X rewards reply engagement and time-on-post (people opening a thread and reading it) more than pure impressions. Native video autoplays and gets weighted reasonably well, but text threads with a strong opening line remain a core format for this audience specifically — sim racing X has an active, opinionated, detail-oriented community culture.

**Format specifics:**
- Threads: strong opening tweet that could stand alone as a complete thought, then depth in the replies
- Quote-card images (file 07's visual spec) perform well as single-tweet posts
- Native video clips (repurposed from Shorts) work but should be trimmed to the platform's attention span — even shorter than the TikTok cut if possible

**Posting cadence & timing:** 1–3/day — X rewards frequency and real-time engagement more than the other platforms; a single high-quality post per day under-utilizes the platform's actual behavior pattern.

**Reply strategy:** This is the single most important platform for direct, real-time engagement with the exact target audience (competitive iRacing drivers are disproportionately active on X within sim racing circles) — budget real time daily for genuine replies to others' posts, not just monitoring your own mentions.

**What performs here specifically:** Building in Public (honest metrics, ship logs, bug posts — file 08's templates were written with X as the primary platform in mind), quote-cards, and technical deep-dive threads (file 08 template 7).

## LinkedIn

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** LinkedIn rewards dwell time on a post (people stopping to actually read) and comments significantly more than likes. First-person, vulnerable, specific founder narrative consistently outperforms polished corporate-style posts on this platform, especially in the indie/small-team SaaS space.

**Format specifics:** Text-first posts with a strong first line (LinkedIn truncates after ~3 lines with a "see more" — that first visible chunk has to earn the click). Native video works but is secondary to text here for this particular content type.

**Posting cadence & timing:** 2–3/week. Weekday mornings (roughly 8–10am in the founder's primary timezone) tend to outperform weekends/evenings on this specifically professional-context platform — validate against real analytics (file 21) rather than assuming.

**What performs here specifically:** Building in Public (file 08) is the primary pillar for LinkedIn — this platform's audience skews toward other founders/operators/professionals who are interested in the business story, not just the racing content. Pure racing-education content underperforms here relative to other platforms; don't force it.

## Facebook

**Algorithm behavior that matters:** Facebook's organic reach for brand pages is structurally the lowest of any platform in this system — its actual value for TrackDelta comes from **Groups**, not the Page feed. Sim racing has a mature, active Facebook Groups ecosystem (league groups, car/track-specific groups, regional groups) that behaves more like old-school forums than an algorithmic feed.

**Format specifics:** Repurpose Reels-format video for the Page; for Groups, native text posts with a direct, non-salesy framing work far better than obvious promotional video drops — see the Reddit section below, the etiquette is nearly identical.

**Posting cadence & timing:** 3–4/week to the Page (low-effort repurposing, not a priority for original content); Group engagement is opportunistic and relationship-based, not calendar-driven — see file 10 §3 for the organic-first approach.

**What performs here specifically:** Weekly roundup posts (linking to the best-performing piece from other platforms) and, in Groups specifically, genuinely helpful direct answers to other members' questions with no promotional framing at all.

## Reddit (new to this system — not previously covered)

**Why Reddit matters for this specific product:** r/simracing and r/iRacing are unusually concentrated with exactly the target persona (competitive iRacing drivers, 1,000–5,000 iRating) and unusually hostile to anything that reads as marketing. Getting Reddit right is high-value; getting it wrong (even once) can permanently poison the well with an entire community.

**Algorithm/culture behavior that matters:** Reddit has no discovery algorithm pushing your content to strangers based on engagement signals the way TikTok/IG do — visibility is driven entirely by upvotes within the specific subreddit, which are driven entirely by the community's genuine judgment of value. There is no way to "trick" Reddit distribution; the only lever is actually being useful.

**The self-promotion rule (non-negotiable):**
1. Do not post about TrackDelta on r/simracing or r/iRacing until you have a real, established, non-promotional posting/commenting history in those communities (minimum several weeks, ideally longer)
2. When you do post about the product, disclose plainly that you're the founder/builder in the post itself — undisclosed self-promotion, once discovered (and it usually is), triggers not just a downvote but often a ban and a public callout
3. Follow the specific subreddit's self-promotion rules to the letter (many require a specific ratio of non-promotional to promotional posts, or restrict promotion to specific days/threads) — check the sidebar/wiki of each subreddit before ever posting
4. Favor genuinely useful posts over announcement posts: "Here's how I approach reading a brake trace, feedback welcome" earns more trust than "Check out my new app"

**Format specifics:** Long-form text posts perform best — Reddit's culture rewards depth and willingness to engage in comment discussion far more than a slick video drop. If posting a video, a native Reddit video or a YouTube link with genuine context in the post body (not just a bare link) is far better received than a bare link alone.

**Best content fit:** Technical deep-dive posts (file 08 template 7 material), honest building-in-public updates, and direct participation in existing threads where someone is asking exactly the kind of question TrackDelta's education content (file 09) already answers well.

**Cadence:** Not a scheduled cadence platform — participate genuinely and consistently (aim for real engagement several times a week), post about the product itself only occasionally and only when there's real news (a meaningful feature ship, a genuine case study, direct response to community feedback).
